Here are some random facts for today, November 5th:
The Gunpowder Plot was foiled on this day in 1605. Early in the morning, the English authorities discovered Guy Fawkes guarding barrels of gunpowder hidden beneath the Houses of Parliament, where he and other Catholic conspirators planned to assassinate King James I and the government. The event is still commemorated annually in Britain as "Bonfire Night" or "Guy Fawkes Day".
Susan B. Anthony was arrested for voting illegally on November 5, 1872. The women's rights activist defiantly cast a ballot in the U.S. presidential election to protest the laws that barred women from voting. She was later tried and fined, an act that brought national attention to the suffrage movement.
The board game Monopoly was first released to the public on this date in 1935.
Franklin D. Roosevelt was elected to an unprecedented third term as U.S. president on November 5, 1940, a feat no president before him had accomplished.
George Foreman became the oldest heavyweight boxing champion in history on November 5, 1994, at the age of 45, defeating Michael Moorer 20 years after losing to Muhammad Ali.
The Android mobile operating system was officially unveiled by Google on November 5, 2007.
